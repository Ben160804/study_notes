## Unit-wise Core Map

### UNIT 1: Research Formulation & Design
Topics:
- Meaning of research
- Objectives of research
- Types of research:
  - basic / applied
  - descriptive / analytical
  - qualitative / quantitative
  - conceptual / empirical
- Research process
- Selection of research problem
- Literature review
- Hypothesis and research questions
- Research design

What to memorize:
1. Research = systematic investigation to discover / verify knowledge
2. Research problem = the exact issue you want to investigate
3. Hypothesis = tentative testable statement
4. Literature review = structured review of prior work
5. Research design = blueprint of the study
6. Difference between qualitative and quantitative research

Best answer pattern:
Definition -> purpose -> steps -> example

---

### UNIT 2: Data Collection, Sampling, Testing
Topics:
- Primary vs secondary data
- Observation, interview, questionnaire, schedule
- Census vs sample
- Sampling methods:
  - probability: simple random, stratified, systematic, cluster
  - non-probability: convenience, judgment, quota
- Data processing and analysis
- Hypothesis testing
- Statistical tools
- SPSS, t-test, chi-square, ANOVA basics

What to memorize:
1. Primary data = collected first-hand
2. Secondary data = already collected by others
3. Sampling = selecting subset representing population
4. Null hypothesis H0 vs alternative hypothesis H1
5. Type I and Type II error
6. t-test = compare means
7. ANOVA = compare more than two means

This unit is process-heavy, not theorem-heavy. Keep definitions clean.

---

### UNIT 3: Ethics, IPR, Publishing
Topics:
- Research ethics
- Plagiarism
- Citation and referencing
- Copyright
- Patent
- Trademark
- IPR basics
- TRIPS
- Scholarly publishing
- IMRAD structure

What to memorize:
1. Research ethics = honesty, integrity, objectivity, proper attribution
2. Plagiarism = presenting others’ work as your own
3. Citation = acknowledging source
4. Patent = exclusive right over invention
5. Copyright = protection of original expression
6. TRIPS = WTO agreement on intellectual property standards
7. IMRAD = Introduction, Methods, Results, and Discussion

This unit is definition-rich and easy to score if terms are crisp.

---

### UNIT 4: Interpretation & Report Writing
Topics:
- Interpretation of data
- Report writing
- Types of reports
- Technical report format
- Oral presentation
- Mechanics of writing
- Bibliography / references
- Annexure / appendix

What to memorize:
1. Interpretation = drawing meaning from analyzed data
2. Good report qualities = clarity, precision, objectivity, logical order
3. Typical report sections:
   - title
   - abstract
   - introduction
   - objectives
   - methodology
   - analysis / results
   - conclusion
   - references
   - appendix
4. Oral presentation qualities = structure, brevity, clarity, confidence

This unit is template-driven. Once you know the structure, answers repeat.
