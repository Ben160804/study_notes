5. What are the roles of the Analysis, Redo and Undo phases in the recovery

algorithm ‘ARIES’? [WBUT 2010, 2011, 2013, 2014, 20

Answer: ;

The ARIES recovery procedure consists of three main steps:

* Analysis: It identifies the dirty (updated) pages in the buffer and the set of
transactions active at the time of crash. The appropriate point in the log where REDO
operation should start is also determined.

* REDO phase: It actually reapplies updates from the log to the database. Generally
the REDO operation is applied to only committed transactions. However, in ARIES,
this is not the case. Certain information in the ARIES log will provide the start point
for REDO, from which REDO operations are applied until the end of the log is
reached. Thus only the necessary REDO operations are applied during recovery.

* UNDO phase: The log is scanned backwards and the operations of transactions that :
were active at the time of the crash are undone in reverse order. The information

' needed for ARIES to accomplish its recovery procedure includes the log, the
transaction table, and the dirty page table. In addition, check pointing is used.
